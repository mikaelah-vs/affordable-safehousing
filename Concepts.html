Concepts

Affordable and Safe Housing

Definition of Terms

Fundamental Human Right: A principle implying that certain standards, such as shelter, are necessary for human existence and should be guaranteed to all regardless of income or status.

Secure Tenure: The legal protection that ensures individuals cannot be evicted from their homes or land arbitrarily; it provides the "right to remain."

Informal Settlements: Areas where housing is constructed on land that the occupants do not have a legal claim to, or where housing does not comply with current planning and building regulations (often called "slums").

Rent-Trap: A situation where a high percentage of an individual's income is spent on rent, making it impossible for them to save enough money to transition to homeownership or invest in their future.

Speculative Real Estate: The practice of purchasing property with the expectation that it will increase in value over time, often leading to price bubbles that make housing unaffordable for the local population.
